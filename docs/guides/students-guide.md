# Student Guide: How to Use PEEL Detective

Welcome to PEEL Detective — an app where you solve crime mysteries by writing structured PEEL paragraphs. An AI evaluates your response and gives you instant scored feedback.

## 1. Creating an Account

1. Go to the app and click **Sign Up** (or visit `/register`).
2. Fill in the form:
   - **Full name** — your real name
   - **Email** — used to log in
   - **Password** — must include uppercase, lowercase, a number, and a special character
   - **Confirm password** — must match
   - **Nickname** *(optional)* — a unique display name (3–30 characters: letters, numbers, `_`, `-`). The app checks availability in real time.
3. Click **Create Account**. You will be taken to your dashboard automatically.

> You can also sign up with **Google** if your teacher allows it — click the Google button instead of filling in the form.

---

## 2. Logging In

1. Go to `/login`.
2. Enter your email and password, then click **Sign In**.
3. The app sends you to your role-based dashboard automatically.

If you forgot your password, click **Forgot password?** on the login page and follow the email instructions.

---

## 3. Your Dashboard

After logging in you land on your **Student Dashboard** (`/dashboard`). Here is what you will find:

| Section | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Total Points** | Points you have earned across all submissions |
| **Cases Solved** | Number of cases you have received AI feedback on |
| **Best Score** | Your highest total score out of 20 |
| **Daily Tip** | A rotating writing tip — changes each day |

Two quick-action buttons are always visible:

- **Open Cases** — takes you to the list of available mysteries
- **Read the Guide** — opens the PEEL Writing Guide

## 4. Learning the PEEL Method

Before solving your first case, read the guide at `/learn`. It explains the four elements of a PEEL paragraph with examples at three quality levels (Basic, Developing, Excellent):

| Letter | Element | What to write |
|---|---|---|
| **P** | **Point** | State your claim — who is the culprit and why? |
| **E** | **Evidence** | Cite specific clues from the crime scene |
| **E** | **Explain** | Connect the clues to your claim with logical reasoning |
| **L** | **Link** | Tie your conclusion back to the scenario question |

A complete response is one flowing paragraph that moves through all four elements. Aim for **80–150 words**.

## 5. Browsing Cases

Go to **Open Cases** or navigate to `/scenarios` to see all available mysteries.

### Reading the case cards

Each card shows:
- **Difficulty** — one to three stars (★ = easier, ★★★ = harder)
- **Status badge** — `Unsolved` if you have not attempted it, or your score (e.g. `✓ 14/20`) if you have
- **Title and description** of the crime

### Filtering by difficulty

Use the filter buttons at the top — **All**, **★**, **★★**, **★★★** — to narrow the list.

### Leaderboard

Scroll down to see the **Top Detectives** leaderboard, ranked by total points.

---

## 6. Investigating a Case

Click **Investigate** on any case card. You will see the full investigation page with:

- **Crime Scene Briefing** — the story and background of the crime
- **Suspects** — profiles of everyone who could be responsible. Click a suspect's image to enlarge it.
- **Evidence Board** — the clues available to you

Read everything carefully before you start writing. When you are ready, click the button at the bottom of the page:

> **Begin Writing Your Case Report →**

---

## 7. Writing Your Case Report

### The writing form

Once you click to begin, a text area appears. Write your PEEL paragraph directly in this box.

**Tips while writing:**

- A word count is shown below the text area. Aim for at least 80 words.
- Use the **panel toggle buttons** (Crime Scene Briefing / Suspects / Evidence Board) to slide open reference panels on the right side of the screen without losing your place.
- A **PEEL Writing Guide** is available below the text area — click **Show** to expand it as a reminder.

### Submitting

When you are happy with your response, click **Submit Case Report**.

The page shows a live progress indicator while the AI evaluates your work:

1. Reading your case report…
2. Analysing Point — who is the culprit?
3. Checking Evidence — clues cited?
4. Reviewing Explanation — logical connection?
5. Evaluating Link — tied back to the question?

This usually takes about 5 seconds. Do not close the page while it is running.

## 8. Understanding Your Feedback

After evaluation you are taken to the **Case Report Results** page.

### Total score

Your score is displayed out of **20** with a star rating. The score is the sum of four individual component scores.

### PEEL breakdown

| Element | Max score | What the AI looks for |
|---|---|---|
| Point | 5 | Clear, direct claim identifying the culprit |
| Evidence | 5 | Specific clues cited from the scenario |
| Explain | 5 | Logical reasoning connecting evidence to claim |
| Link | 5 | Strong closing statement tied to the question |

Each component shows a status badge:

- **Needs Work** (0–2) — red
- **Developing** (3–4) — amber
- **Excellent** (5) — green

Below each score is the AI's written feedback explaining what was strong and what could be improved.

### Writing notes

If the AI noticed grammar or style issues, they appear as badges in the **Writing Notes** section.

### Model answer

Click **Show model answer** to see an example of an ideal response for the scenario. Use this to understand what an excellent answer looks like.

### What to do next

| Button | Action |
|---|---|
| **Revise My Answer** | Go back and submit a new attempt on the same case |
| **New Case** | Browse a different mystery |
| **My Results** | View your full submission history |

You can attempt the same case as many times as you like. Each attempt is saved and labelled (e.g. "Attempt 2 of 3").

## 9. Tracking Your Progress

### Submission history

Go to `/submissions` (or click **My Results** on the feedback page) to see all your past case reports sorted by date. Each entry shows:

- The case name and submission date
- Status: **Evaluated**, **Pending**, or **Failed**
- Your score out of 20 and the per-element breakdown
- A **View Feedback** button to re-read the AI feedback

### Points and badges

Points are awarded automatically when a submission is evaluated. You can see your total on the dashboard and on the leaderboard. Badges are awarded for milestones and displayed on your profile page (`/profile`).

### Score trend

Your profile page also shows a **score trend chart** so you can see how your writing is improving over time.

---

## 10. Subscriptions

Some cases require an active subscription to submit a response. If your school has set up subscriptions:

- **Unsubscribed** — you can still read the crime briefing and clues, but the **Submit** button will redirect you to the subscription page.
- **Subscribed** — full access to all cases; your renewal date is shown on the dashboard.
- **Free cases** — a small number of cases are always free to attempt, shown with a green **Free** badge.

Contact your teacher if you have questions about access.
